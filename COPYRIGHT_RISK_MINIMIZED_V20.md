# FateCard.today v20 — Copyright-risk-minimized deck policy

This version intentionally avoids the dangerous “small tweak” approach.

## What was changed

- Removed RWS-like figurative scenes from generated SVG cards.
- Replaced them with original symbolic emblem cards.
- Kept only traditional tarot concepts: card names, arcana, suits, numbers, symbolic categories.
- Avoided copying scene composition, human poses, distinctive backgrounds, card-back patterns, or modern deck styling.
- Avoided copying existing tarot website/book interpretation sentences.

## Why not “just slightly different”?

Minor visual changes to a protected or recognizable work can still be risky.
The safer approach is substantial transformation:
traditional idea and symbolic vocabulary remain, but concrete expression is new.

## Current standard

- Public-domain tarot tradition may inspire the symbolic system.
- All image files in `/public/cards` are original symbolic SVG designs for FateCard.today.
- Reading text should remain independently written and should not be copied from commercial tarot sites, apps, books, or modern decks.
- Do not use prompts such as “make it like Rider-Waite,” “copy Smith-Waite,” “in the style of [modern deck],” or “same composition but slightly changed.”
